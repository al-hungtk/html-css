The Evolution of the Bicycle

The First Bicylce

In 1817 Baron von Drais invented a wallking machine that would help him get around the royal gardens faster: two same- size in-line wheels, the front one steerable, mounted in a frame upon which you straddled. The deviece was propelled by pushing your feet against the ground, thus rolling yourself and the device forward in a sort of gliding walk.

The machine bacame known as the Draisienne (or "hobby horse"). It was made entirely of wodd. This enjoyed a short lived popularity as a fad, not being pracitical for transoirtion in any other place than a well maintained pathway such as in a park or garden.

Further Innovation

The next appearance of a two-wheeled riding machine was in 1865., when pedels were applied driectly to the front wheel. This machine was known as the velocipede (meaning "fast foot") as well as the "bone shaker", since it's wooden structure combined with the cobbleestone roads of the day made for an extremely uncomfortavle ride. They also became a fad and indoor riding academies, similar to rller rinks, could be found in large cities.

In 1870 the first all-metal machine appeared. The pedals were atttached directly to the front wheel. Solid rubber tires and the long sopkes of the large front wheel provided a much smoother ride than its perdecessor.
